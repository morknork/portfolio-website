About

A no-life degenerate gamer turned productive member of society. My time playing competitive games was where I found lifelong friends, a fierce drive to be best, and an enduring habit to forget about bedtime until 3am.

Unfortunately, we must all grow up eventually. After some time in customer service, I realised that I’m only happy when believing the work I do is both meaningful and actively attempts to make a difference in people’s lives.

I now find myself as a security analyst in critical infrastructure, work I find important, with people who I’d enjoy spending time with regardless of remuneration. Day to day I lead our cyber awareness activities (phishing simulation training and broader cyber awareness comms), risk assess new and updated systems, monitor security alerts, investigate and support incident response when needed.

I initially built a homelab as just a media server but it’s constantly growing. My latest project is an AD detection lab, running attack sims, observing behaviour, building rules to catch these spectral rogues before they can do any theoretical damage.

I love learning how things work, and building things that protect people seems like a valuable way to spend my time.
